First Named Storm of the Atlantic Hurricane Season, 1950–2026

Data source: NOAA Atlantic Hurricane Database (HURDAT2), 1950–2017, supplemented with NHC records for 2018–2026. Chart shows the formation date of the first named tropical storm or hurricane (sustained winds ≥ 39 mph) of each Atlantic season. Two carryover storms were excluded: Alice (1955) and Zeta (2006), both of which formed in the preceding season and were still active on January 1. Note that naming conventions changed over time: from 1950–1952, storms used the Joint Army/Navy Phonetic Alphabet (Able, Baker, Charlie); female-only names were used from 1953–1978; alternating male and female names have been used since 1979. The dashed line marks June 28, the average first-storm date calculated across all 77 seasons shown (1950–2026).
